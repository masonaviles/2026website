mason.os — two directions to react to.

Both prototypes share the same content, hero shape, and gamification scaffolding. They differ on aesthetic register — one calmer and Apple-leaning, one denser and IDE-leaning. Open both, click around, then tell me which one I should refine (or whether you want a hybrid).

A · macOS — calm, glass, Apple-leaning

light

Frosted panels on a soft gradient, generous spacing, restrained motion. Recruiters land here and immediately feel the Apple bar without anything shouting.

glassmorphism Inter soft motion avatar card "now playing"

B · IDE — dense, terminal, dev-leaning

dark

Window chrome, file-tree rail, tabs, gutter line numbers, IDE status bar. Feels like opening your portfolio inside VSCode. Loud-and-proud "this person ships."

JetBrains Mono tabs · gutter terminal hero achievements grid git status bar

What both prototypes share

  • Hero panel · 12+ year status chip · summary · CTAs (Download résumé + Contact) · icon links (GitHub · Email · Blog · LinkedIn)
  • Persistent left rail (workspace as ~/profile, ~/work, ~/projects, ~/blog, ~/system, ~/playground, ~/contact)
  • Persistent status bar (last deploy, Lighthouse, GitHub commits, achievement progress, "powered by Claude")
  • Achievement system — toast pops in after first paint, unlocks hello_world
  • ⌘K command palette hint, keyboard shortcut affordances, reduced-motion friendly

Where they differ

  • A leads with calm, prestige, and brand. Better for design-leaning recruiters and creative directors.
  • B leads with technical density and "shipped." Better for engineering-leaning hiring managers and staff/principal screens.
  • A hybrid is possible: A's calm hero up top, B's dense panels for ~/system and ~/playground.
